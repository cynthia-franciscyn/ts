Types of Ceramic Coating Available in Bangalore

Standard SiO₂ Ceramic Coating

The most common ceramic coating. Silicon dioxide–based, with 9H hardness rating. Excellent gloss, hydrophobic properties, and UV protection. Lasts 2–3 years with proper care. Best value for daily driver cars.

Graphene Ceramic Coating

The latest generation of ceramic coating, incorporating graphene oxide alongside SiO₂. Graphene coatings offer superior thermal resistance (critical in Bangalore's heat), reduced water spotting compared to standard ceramic coatings, and improved flexibility that makes them less prone to micro-cracking. At Top Secret Detailing Studio, we apply CarPro and Artdeshine graphene coatings — trusted by professional detailers worldwide. Lasts 4–5 years.

Multi-Layer Ceramic Systems

For maximum protection, a base coat followed by one or more top coats is applied. Each additional layer adds depth, hardness, and longevity. Recommended for luxury or performance cars where appearance and resale value are paramount.

How Ceramic Coating Is Applied — Step by Step

  1. Paint decontamination: The car is foam-washed, clay-barred to remove embedded contaminants (iron particles, tar), and chemically decontaminated. This stage is non-negotiable — any contamination trapped under a ceramic coating will remain there permanently.
  2. Paint correction (if required): Swirl marks, scratches, and oxidation are polished out using machine polishers. You cannot seal imperfections in; they must be corrected first.
  3. IPA panel wipe: Every panel is wiped with isopropyl alcohol to remove any polish oils, ensuring a bare, clean surface for bonding.
  4. Coating application: The ceramic coating is applied panel by panel using suede applicators, worked into the surface, and allowed to flash (cure briefly) before buffing to a high gloss.
  5. Initial curing: The vehicle sits for 24–48 hours at controlled humidity and temperature. No water contact during this period.
  6. Full cure: The coating achieves maximum hardness over 7–14 days. During this period, avoid car washes and parking under trees.

How to Maintain Ceramic Coating in Bangalore

  • First 7 days: Keep the car dry, avoid car washes, bird droppings, or parking under trees.
  • Washing: Use a pH-neutral car shampoo (never dish soap or petrol station auto washes). The two-bucket wash method prevents reintroducing swirls.
  • Drying: Use a clean microfiber drying towel. Never rub dry — always blot or drag.
  • Ceramic top-up: Apply a SiO₂ maintenance spray every 3–6 months to refresh the hydrophobic layer.
  • Annual inspection: Visit your detailing studio annually to assess the coating's condition. Minor correction can extend its life significantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does ceramic coating last in Bangalore's climate?
A professionally applied ceramic coating lasts 3–5 years in Bangalore's climate with basic maintenance. Graphene coatings can last longer due to superior heat and moisture resistance — both relevant in Bangalore.
Is ceramic coating worth it in Bangalore?
Yes — emphatically. Bangalore's dust, monsoon acid rain, high UV altitude, and dense traffic make it one of the hardest environments for car paint in India. A single monsoon season can cause significant water spot etching on unprotected paint. The cost of ceramic coating is far less than a panel respray.
Can ceramic coating be applied on a new car?
Yes — and ideally, it should be. New cars often still carry factory swirls from transportation. A light machine polish followed by ceramic coating on a new car is the best way to preserve its value from day one.
Does ceramic coating prevent scratches?
Ceramic coating significantly reduces light scratches and swirl marks from washing and everyday use due to its 9H hardness. It will not prevent deep scratches from keys or stone chips — for that level of physical protection, Paint Protection Film (PPF) is required.
